TITLE: El Niño forecast models point toward an "extreme" event
URL: https://www.cnews.fr/monde/2026-07-07/el-nino-les-modeles-de-prevision-laissent-entrevoir-un-evenement-extreme-1887581

CNews reports that forecast models are pointing toward a potentially extreme El Niño episode in 2026. The article presents El Niño as a climate phenomenon capable of disrupting weather patterns far beyond the tropical Pacific, with a stronger event increasing the likelihood of heat waves, droughts, heavy rain, floods and other extreme weather events.

The report says the phenomenon is expected to reach level 3 out of 4 in forecast intensity, which would raise the probability of severe climate impacts. It frames the situation as a warning signal for governments and populations exposed to weather volatility, because a strong El Niño can shift rainfall patterns, worsen drought risk in some regions and increase flooding risk in others.

The central story is that forecasters are no longer discussing a weak or routine El Niño. Instead, the models leave open the possibility of an unusually strong episode, with consequences that could be amplified by the background trend of human-driven climate warming.
