TITLE: El Niño: Impacts worldwide
URL: https://medeor.de/en/news/close-up/431-el-nino-impacts-worldwide.html

Action medeor describes El Niño through its humanitarian consequences, focusing on droughts, floods, crop failures, displacement, hunger and disease risks in vulnerable countries. The article highlights Somalia, where heavy flooding followed years of drought; floodwater could not easily soak into hardened soil, damaging harvests and contributing to displacement. The organization says hundreds of thousands of people lost their homes and that food shortages and infectious disease risks increased.

The article explains El Niño as a recurring climate phenomenon that weakens trade winds and disrupts weather, especially across many southern hemisphere countries. It also points to Tanzania, where heavy rainfall displaced people, caused landslides and increased epidemic risks after wells and latrines were flooded. Medeor says cholera had broken out in several Tanzanian regions.

The report adds that the effects persist even when El Niño weakens. In Guatemala, unusually low rainfall delayed sowing because farmers feared crops would dry out again. Medeor links this to malnutrition, especially among young children, and says affected populations need clean water, medicine, food, seeds, medical care, disaster preparedness and climate-adapted agriculture.
