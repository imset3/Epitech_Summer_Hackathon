TITLE: This year's El Niño likely to become a record-breaker, top expert says
URL: https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20260707-this-year-s-el-nino-likely-to-become-record-breaker-top-expert

France24 carries an AFP report saying the current El Niño is likely to become record-breaking in its overall strength. The article cites Tim Stockdale, an El Niño specialist at the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts, who says forecast models are unusually strong and consistent in pointing toward an extreme event.

The article explains that El Niño warms the central and eastern equatorial Pacific, changing global wind, pressure and rainfall patterns. It normally occurs every two to seven years and can last around nine to twelve months. A strong episode can contribute to droughts in some areas and flooding in others.

The report links this year's forecast to broader climate risk. El Niño generally peaks between November and February, while its full global temperature effect often appears later. Combined with human-caused climate change, strong El Niño events can help push global temperatures higher. The story also mentions risks for agriculture, wildfires, floods, landslides, monsoon disruption and humanitarian preparedness across Asia, Africa, South America and Australia.
